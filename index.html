Welcome to the Canadian Neuromorphic Engineering Workshop

"The brain is imagination, and that was exciting to me; I wanted to build a chip that could imagine something" - Misha Mahowald

About the Workshop

Join us for an exciting convergence of minds exploring the intersection of neuroscience, engineering, and artificial intelligence. This workshop brings together researchers, engineers, and enthusiasts from diverse backgrounds to share insights, collaborate, and push the boundaries of neuromorphic computing.

What to Expect

Engage with cutting-edge research in event-driven sensing, spiking neural networks, neuromorphic hardware platforms, and bio-inspired algorithms. Whether you're a neuroscientist curious about computational models, an engineer interested in brain-inspired systems, or a researcher exploring the next generation of intelligent machines, this workshop offers something for everyone.

Workshop Topics

  • Neuromorphic hardware platforms (SpiNNaker, Loihi, and emerging technologies)
  • Event-based vision and sensing
  • Spiking neural networks and learning algorithms
  • Bio-inspired robotics and autonomous systems
  • Real-time processing and ultra-low-power computation
  • Neuromorphic applications in robotics, AI, and beyond
  • Brain-inspired algorithms and architectures
  • Sensorimotor integration and active perception
